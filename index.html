Effective Duopoly: RESCUE! + Spectracide account for 97%+ of Lure units every single month. Landisun holds a steady 2–3% with a cheap 2-pack ($9.99) but has no path to meaningful share without a price-competitive multi-pack.
Spectracide's Seasonal Window: Spectracide surged to 40–45% share Aug–Oct 2025 — nearly equalling RESCUE! in peak season. Their 1-pack trap ($14.97) captures first-time buyers at the start of wasp season. Outside peak months their share collapses to 4–14%.
RESCUE! Off-Season Dominance: RESCUE! commands 79–94% share in low-season months (Mar, Jun, Jan, Feb) when repeat buyers and refill purchases drive volume. High share in low-volume months underscores deep brand loyalty and a broad product ecosystem (traps + refills).
Entry Opportunity: No brand has successfully established consistent share in the 3-pack or 6-pack formats — both are 98–100% RESCUE!. A competitively priced multi-pack from a new entrant targeting mid-season (May–Jul) could carve out share before RESCUE! and Spectracide dominate the Aug–Oct peak.

Narrative Patterns — 5 most common customer stories (1★+2★+3★)

PATTERN 1  ·  "Trap catches nothing despite visible wasp population"
Hundreds of yellowjackets in yard — trap sits empty for weeks
  • Customer has an obvious yellowjacket or wasp problem near their home
  • Sets up trap correctly near nest or feeding area
  • Returns after 1–3 weeks to find trap nearly empty
  • Can still see wasps swarming nearby — trap clearly not attracting them
"I watched a video showing hundreds caught. Despite hundreds around my property, trap caught 3 in 2 weeks."  ·  "Hanging 4 feet from an active nest — 8 total yellowjackets in 3 weeks."
→ 57% of negative reviews — effectiveness proof is the #1 purchase barrier in this category
PATTERN 2  ·  "Killing bees and hummingbirds — not the target pest"
Trap fills with honeybees and beneficial insects, zero yellowjackets
  • Customer sets up trap to eliminate wasp problem
  • Discovers the trap is catching honeybees, bumblebees, hummingbirds, or butterflies
  • Immediately angry — harming protected or beneficial species
  • Removes trap and leaves a 1★ review warning others
"Killed 12 honeybees and zero yellowjackets."  ·  "I found a hummingbird drowned in the trap — never using this product again."
→ 24% of neg reviews — a "bee-safe" or species-selective design is a major market opportunity
PATTERN 3  ·  "Wrong geographic formula — Mountain vs. Eastern"
Attractant not matched to local yellowjacket species — zero catch
  • Customer orders product without noticing the regional formula distinction
  • Receives Mountain & Pacific formula for an Eastern location (or vice versa)
  • Attractant chemistry targets different wasp species — local wasps ignore it
  • Customer concludes product doesn't work — unaware the issue is geographic mismatch
"Ordered for Texas — received Mountain & Pacific formula. Caught nothing all summer."  ·  "This formula is for the West. I'm in Virginia. Zero results."
→ 10% of neg reviews — regional formula labeling and discovery filters are a clear product/listing improvement
PATTERN 4  ·  "Works initially — population returns from the nest"
Catches wasps for a few days, then colony replenishes
  • Customer installs trap — it works, catches wasps in the first 1–3 days
  • Expected the trap to eliminate the nest or colony — it doesn't
  • Yellowjacket population rebounds within days from the ground nest
  • Customer frustrated: "caught some, but I still have the same problem"
"Catches plenty at first, then they stop. The nest is still there."  ·  "Problem returned within a week — didn't address the source."
→ Trap-only strategy is insufficient for heavy infestations — listing should set expectations and offer a complementary nest treatment
PATTERN 5  ·  "Wildlife and pets at risk — safety concern overrides effectiveness"
Liquid attractant poses risk to dogs, cats, birds, and children
  • Customer sets up trap outdoors near areas where pets or wildlife are present
  • Dog drinks from trap liquid, cat bats it down, bird drowns — product becomes a hazard
  • Customer prioritises safety and removes the product regardless of wasp performance
  • Review focuses entirely on the safety failure, often warning pet owners away
"My dog drank the bait liquid before I could stop him — had to call the vet."  ·  "Found a songbird drowned inside. Took it down immediately."
→ 5% of neg reviews — a sealed design with no accessible liquid would eliminate both the wildlife and pet safety failure modes
